
### Who is roughly in each decile? 

| IMD decile | Plain-world bucket                          | Who this tends to look like in real life                                                                                                                                             |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1          | **Hard estates / crisis-heavy areas**       | Long-term unemployed, long-term sick, people in social housing, single parents under pressure, people cycling through benefits, insecure work, rougher health, more visible disorder |
| 2          | **Poor estates and very low-paid streets**  | Cleaners, carers, warehouse workers, kitchen porters, delivery work, agency work, people between jobs, crowded housing, many households with several problems at once                |
| 3          | **Low-paid working-class areas**            | Shop workers, security guards, factory/logistics workers, care workers, hotel staff, call-centre staff, council tenants and private renters mixed together                           |
| 4          | **Ordinary working-class streets**          | Drivers, bin crews, chefs, junior trades, NHS support staff, teaching assistants, council workers, retail supervisors, renters and some homeowners                                   |
| 5          | **Mixed lower-middle / working households** | Builders, mechanics, experienced care workers, admin staff, nursery workers, chefs, lower-paid office workers, younger families, stretched homeowners and renters                    |
| 6          | **Stable ordinary suburbs**                 | Nurses, teachers, police staff, firefighters, paramedics, skilled trades, office workers, school staff, small business owners, mostly stable households                              |
| 7          | **Secure working / middle-class suburbs**   | Experienced teachers, nurses with two-income households, established trades, police/fire, mid-level managers, civil servants, homeowners, families with some cushion                 |
| 8          | **Professional suburbs and commuter towns** | Engineers, accountants, project managers, senior public-sector workers, head teachers, trades business owners, consultants, comfortable homeowners                                   |
| 9          | **Affluent professional areas**             | Doctors, lawyers, finance workers, architects, senior managers, academics, business owners, large-home suburbs, affluent villages                                                    |
| 10         | **Elite / insulated areas**                 | Executives, partners, senior consultants, wealthy retirees, major business owners, landlords, expensive rural villages, private-school / high-asset households                       |

For chart labels, I’d use this:

**Hard estates / crisis-heavy**
**Poor estates / very low-paid**
**Low-paid working class**
**Ordinary working class**
**Mixed lower-middle**
**Stable ordinary suburbs**
**Secure working / middle class**
**Professional suburbs**
**Affluent professionals**
**Elite / insulated**

**Decile 7 is not “barely comfortable.”** It is already a noticeably secure area: more owner-occupation, more stable families, fewer households with overlapping problems, fewer visible signs of deprivation. Teachers and nurses can absolutely be there, but usually as part of stable two-income households, older homeowners, or senior/experienced workers. The same jobs can sit lower when renting, single-income, younger, in an expensive city, or surrounded by a poorer local mix.
